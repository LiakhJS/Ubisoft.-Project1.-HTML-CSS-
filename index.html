about

ubisoft

The five Guillemot brothers founded Ubisoft in 1986, and soon a band of brothers became a worldwide network of 20000 people who grow and learn together.

Mauris these family roots have helped shape Ubisoft today, encouraging all team members to dream big, embrace the entrepreneurial spirit and work to create an open and welcoming environment where everyone is respected . Ubisoft's expansion to a worldwide network of studios and offices spanning 55 countries began in Romania, Canada and China throughout the 1990s. Establishing an international presence was key to keeping up with the gaming industry's explosive growth.

books

Ubisoft is expanding its network of publishing partners to create more Assassin's Creed-based content in these formats featuring a variety of authors, illustrators, and content creators from all over the world in the hope that more stories resonate with fans wherever they live — particularly in China, Japan, and Korea.

"With so many creators from all over Asia delivering innovative content, a priority for Ubisoft has been to forge new relationships with talented partners in the region," says Julien Fabre, associate director of publishing at Ubisoft. "We are all consumers of stories," adds Etienne Bouvier, publishing content manager at Ubisoft. "With this global vision and local approach, we seek to offer our fans new content in formats they are familiar with, and with stories that are rooted in their cultural background and their history."

music

Music has always been a crucial element in any game. Accompanying you on your journey through Dark Age England will be the music of composers Jesper Kyd and Sarah Schachner. Joining these series veterans will be norwegian musician Einar Selvik, who will create original songs for the soundtrack.

A Bafta award-winning danish composer, Jesper Kyd composed the soundtrack for the original Assassin's creed. His most recent composing credits include Borderlands 3, State Of Decay 2, and Warhammer: Vermintide 2. Also known by his stage name Kvitrafn, Einar Selvik is a multi-instrumental norwegian artist. Sarah Schachner is an american composer known for blending authentic folk string instruments with analog synths, electronic production, and orchestra.
